Work About Resume

One of the biggest challenges to eating at home is cooking. This is because people either may not have time to decide on a meal they’d like to try or they can’t figure out what ingredients are necessary for meals. People also want to save time and money. But oftentimes, they find themselves spending more time trying to decide what to eat rather than cooking meals. This leads to eating out and spending more money on groceries and take out. Overall, wasting time, money and food.

Research

Objectives

We wanted to understand what were the challenges when it comes to feeding a family. We all had our experiences as to why it could be difficult to meal plan for our own lifestyles but we kept brainstorming as we began our research. In order to conduct our research precisely to our goal, we pinpointed our objectives which are the following:

  • Identify most users' current meal planning habits
  • Understand users typical food preferences and tastes
  • Discover user pain points of feeding a family on a weekly basis

Thus, we began our competitor analysis to see how we can develop our app and see what is currently in the market.

Competitor Analysis

We began investigating apps who were mobile, popular amongst the app store, and had similar features among one another. We assessed features to identify how we can break out of the mold and provide features that can stand out on their own while still providing fundamental necessities our users require.

Proto Persona

To understand who Pantry users are, we began with sending a survey questionnaire with demographics and general questions about meal planning and cooking. With the results from the survey, we were able to create a persona. This enabled us to understand our users' needs based on their gains and pain points.

User Interviews

We sent a preliminary survey in order to gauge our users along with creating our proto persona then we used it to determine who would fit our criteria for interviews. With a user in mind, we created a research interview plan that had open-ended questions that developed from our main objectives to provide us a deeper insight to our user’s motivations and psyche. Thus we started to recruit our participants for our interview. We recruited five participants to interview that enabled us to gain insight to confirm if our initial comprehension of our users were accurate from our survey and proto persona. We discovered the following insights:

  • Most reuse their dinners for tomorrow’s lunch
  • Having a shopping list with food listed by aisle is desirable to our users
  • Most decide what to eat by process of elimination
  • Most don’t know what they have already in their pantry when they go shopping
  • Time is a common issue
  • Most plan dinner last minute


After our interviews, we gathered our plan and notes to begin defining and ideating Pantry.

Define & Ideate

We began by synthesizing data from our survey and interviews to help us to identify how we can resolve the challenges facing our users goal's when it comes to meal planning.

Affinity Diagram

We organized our interview insights using an Affinity Diagram that included demographics and snippets of comments from our participants. We inspected our diagram for common patterns between our users and organized the notes by sorting them into groups.

Empathy Map

After grouping our insights we wanted to gain a deeper understanding of how our user’s empathize with their meal planning. We used an empathy map to distinguish our user’s modes of perception and how they connect with meal planning and how we can create solutions for these perceptions.

From our deduced data, we discovered the needs for our site to address both pains and gains expressed from our user. However, we desired to stay centered on our specific user so we began with composing a user persona.


User Persona

With the insight from our empathy map and affinity diagram, We wanted to ensure we had a focal user as we began to ideate and know who to design for. We created our user persona Leann Simone Genovese!

Now with a better understanding of who our Pantry user is, their pains and gains. We gathered our top features and began to dissect which features will provide our user with their needs.

Feature Prioritization Matrix

We wanted to ensure we were prioritizing features that were beneficial to our users and available per our capacity to develop into Pantry. With understanding what the sense our users have per our Empathy Map we knew how we can create solutions for these perceptions but how that looked like Feature wise is why we develop our feature Prioritization to see if it is feasible as well.

After we organized the features we wanted to center Pantry for our users, so we started sketching our ideas.

Lo-fi Wireframe Sketches

Our wireframe sketches were on the main pages our users would interact with. Before ideating the rest of Pantry, we had to ensure we knew the steps our users would take to accomplish their goals in Pantry. Thus, we started mapping a user flow.

User Flow

In order to understand the journey of our users, we needed to see the steps they would take while interacting with Pantry. It was complex based on different tasks that our users would try to accomplish. We iterated three times before we reached our final mapping to ensure the flow was intuitive and we could provide a design that provides all the content they need to interact with Pantry.

Prototyping & Testing

To test the functionality of our design, we conducted usability testing early into our prototype to ensure we could refine our app and iterate our final prototype to be functional and intuitive to our users.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes & Prototype

Starting our prototype began with creating our wireframes using Adobe XD.Once we had created our wireframes, we used Adobe XD to make our limited functional prototype.

Usability Testing

Our usability testing was done remote via Zoom with a moderated Think Aloud method. We recruited five participants to test our design’s functionality by stating their thoughts on how they felt, what they saw, and their actions while going testing the site by completing tasks given. We formulated tasks based on our main pages which were our homepage, cravings quiz, meal calendar and recipes. We used Miroboard to take notes of the tasks and notes from feedback and repeated for our interactions and notes for revisions.The following is the feedback received:

  • Fix craving quiz to go to next questions
  • Remove plus button
  • Adjust cravings quiz to home page
  • Fix link to cravings quiz
  • Discovered extra step to meal plan process to eliminate

Revisions

We had three iterations per the feedback that we received.It was vital to ensure our app was friendly and functional due to the complexity of our problem. We wanted to ensure our app was intuitive and friendly which had us going back and iterating our user flow as well. We used our feedback and made revisions. Our final usability testing phase reception was considered great among our users testing and provided additional recommendations on how to grow Pantry with additional features. The following were all our revisions per our feedback:

  • Fix craving quiz to go to next questions
  • Remove plus button
  • Adjust cravings quiz to home page
  • Fix link to cravings quiz
  • Discovered extra step to meal plan process to eliminate
  • Add skip button to onboarding
  • Add home button instead of logo for home function
  • Broken “like” feature
  • Fix "Eat out" section to suggestions instead of recipes
  • Add more pictures

Once we had refined our flow and general wireframe layout. We began branding Pantry.

Branding

Before applying UI design to our wireframes, we wanted to ensure that the site had a theme with precise motif and continuity. We began with food and meal planning but needed to narrow our brand to a specific theme to ensure there wasn't an overlap with our competitors and other apps that relate to just food.

Mood Board

We began seeking inspiration via Google search images and Pinterest. We searched for pantry, planners, grocery lists, recipe/recipe lists and food organization. Along with food icons and colors that can present the tone of Pantry which we sought for a modern, clean, organized, and warm.

Logo Design

Our logo was based on our color scheme white and orange and it provided a clean yet organized aesthetic.

UI Style Guide

After designing our logo and solidifying elements of the Pantry brand. We created our style guide with the intention to complement our key UI elements. Then we applied our style guide to our wireframes.

Final Prototype

Prototype

After revising our wireframes and applying UI design, we implemented our design via development and finalized our prototype.
